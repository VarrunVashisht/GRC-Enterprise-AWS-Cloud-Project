# Incident Impact Analysis  
## IAM Credential Compromise & Ransomware Deployment  
### Southern Cross Capital Pty Ltd – Executive Impact Review

---

# 1️⃣ Purpose

This document evaluates the business, financial, operational, and regulatory impact of the simulated ransomware incident.

In financial services, incident impact analysis must go beyond technical damage.

It must assess:

- Financial exposure
- Regulatory obligations
- Reputation risk
- Governance escalation
- Risk register implications

---

# 2️⃣ Financial Impact Analysis

---

## Direct Financial Loss

- Trading platform outage during market hours
- Lost transaction revenue
- Emergency response costs
- Forensic investigation expenses

Estimated impact (simulated mid-sized firm):

- Revenue loss: Moderate to High
- Incident response & recovery cost: Significant
- Infrastructure rebuild cost: Moderate

---

## Customer Compensation Risk

If customers experienced financial trading loss due to outage:

- Potential compensation claims
- Dispute resolution costs
- Possible legal exposure

In financial institutions, operational downtime has direct monetary consequences.

---

# 3️⃣ Operational Impact

---

## Service Disruption

- Trading platform unavailable
- Customer portal inaccessible
- Support team overwhelmed with complaints

Operational downtime affects:

- Customer confidence
- Market credibility
- Business continuity objectives

---

## Business Continuity Evaluation

Incident tests:

- Backup effectiveness
- Disaster recovery capability
- RTO (Recovery Time Objective)
- RPO (Recovery Point Objective)

Failure to meet RTO/RPO increases governance exposure.

---

# 4️⃣ Regulatory Impact (Australia)

---

## Privacy Act Implications

If customer personal information was accessed or exfiltrated:

- Mandatory breach notification under Notifiable Data Breaches (NDB) Scheme
- Notification to Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC)
- Customer notification requirement

Failure to notify may result in penalties.

---

## APRA CPS 234 Implications

APRA CPS 234 requires:

- Effective information security controls
- Board accountability
- Incident reporting to APRA if material

A ransomware incident impacting production systems would likely qualify as a material incident.

Board-level review required.

---

## Regulatory Consequences

Potential outcomes:

- Increased regulatory scrutiny
- Compliance review
- Mandated remediation plan
- Reputational oversight

Financial institutions operate under stricter incident reporting obligations than most industries.

---

# 5️⃣ Reputational Impact

Reputation damage can exceed technical damage.

Potential effects:

- Customer trust erosion
- Media attention
- Investor concern
- Competitive disadvantage

In financial services, trust is core business capital.

Even temporary outage may reduce customer retention.

---

# 6️⃣ Risk Register Update

Following incident analysis:

---

## Risk Reassessment

- R2 – IAM Privilege Escalation likelihood increased
- R3 – Ransomware exposure validated as realistic
- Monitoring effectiveness reviewed

---

## Control Enhancement Recommendations

- Phishing-resistant MFA (hardware token)
- Privileged Access Management (PAM)
- Automated IAM anomaly detection
- Backup immutability enforcement
- Regular ransomware simulation exercises

---

## Residual Risk Adjustment

Likelihood for IAM compromise may increase if human phishing risk remains high.

Control investment required to reduce probability further.

---

# 7️⃣ Executive Escalation Logic

Given:

- Production outage
- Financial service disruption
- Potential regulatory breach

Escalation level:

Executive Leadership → Board → Legal & Compliance → Regulator (if required)

Incident classified as:

Critical Severity – Regulatory Relevant

---

# 8️⃣ Governance Maturity Reflection

This incident demonstrates:

- Identity is highest cloud risk domain
- Backup governance must include immutability
- Monitoring reduces dwell time but not impact
- Human factor remains primary vulnerability

Security maturity requires:

Preventive controls  
Detective controls  
Corrective controls  
Executive oversight  

---

# 9️⃣ Strategic Conclusion

In financial services:

Cyber incidents are not IT events.  
They are business risk events.

Impact spans:

Financial  
Operational  
Regulatory  
Reputational  

Incident response capability directly affects regulatory trust and market confidence.

Security governance must assume breach and design resilience accordingly.
