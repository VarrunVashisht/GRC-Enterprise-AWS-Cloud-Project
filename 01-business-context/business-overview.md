# Business Overview  
## Southern Cross Capital Pty Ltd

---

## 1. Company Overview

**Company Name:** Southern Cross Capital Pty Ltd  
**Industry:** Financial Services (Australia)  
**Type:** Mid-Sized Investment & Wealth Management Firm  

Southern Cross Capital Pty Ltd is a mid-sized Australian financial services organisation providing digital investment and wealth management solutions to retail and corporate clients across Australia. The company manages approximately 75,000 active customer accounts and operates primarily through online platforms.

### Core Services

- Retail investment accounts  
- Managed wealth portfolios  
- Online trading platform  
- Payroll-linked automated investment accounts  
- Digital financial advisory services  

The company relies heavily on secure digital infrastructure to deliver real-time trading, portfolio management, and secure transaction processing.

Because financial data is highly sensitive and directly tied to customer assets, cybersecurity is considered a **core business function**, not just an IT responsibility.

---

## 2. Business Operations

Southern Cross Capital operates through a combination of customer-facing applications and internal administrative systems. The organisation is currently migrating key systems from on-premises infrastructure to Amazon Web Services (AWS) to improve scalability, resilience, and operational efficiency.

### Core Systems Being Migrated to AWS

1. **Online Trading Web Application**
   - Customer login portal
   - Portfolio dashboard
   - Trade execution system
   - API integrations with market data providers

2. **Customer Financial Records Database**
   - Personal identity information (PII)
   - Bank account details
   - Investment balances
   - Transaction history

3. **Payment Processing System**
   - Bank transfer integrations
   - Payroll-linked recurring investments
   - Settlement processing

4. **Identity Verification & Authentication System**
   - Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
   - Credential management
   - Access logging

5. **Internal Administrative Systems**
   - HR platform
   - Payroll management
   - Employee credential management
   - Internal audit documentation

The migration to AWS introduces new cloud-related risks including misconfiguration, identity mismanagement, excessive permissions, data exposure, and cloud account compromise. Therefore, security architecture and risk governance must be embedded into the migration strategy.

## 3. Regulatory & Risk Environment (Australian Context)

As a financial services provider operating in Australia, Southern Cross Capital must comply with multiple regulatory and legal obligations.

### Key Regulatory Requirements

- **Australian Privacy Act**
  - Protection of personal information
  - Data breach notification obligations

- **APRA CPS 234 (Information Security)**
  - Mandatory information security controls
  - Board-level accountability for cyber risk
  - Ongoing security capability assessment

- **Notifiable Data Breaches (NDB) Scheme**
  - Mandatory reporting of eligible data breaches

- **Financial transaction integrity and fraud prevention standards**
  - Protection of financial transaction systems
  - Monitoring and detection of fraudulent activity

Failure to meet these obligations can result in regulatory penalties, legal action, operational restrictions, and reputational damage.

In this environment, cybersecurity risk must be actively identified, measured, mitigated, and continuously monitored.

Cyber risk is therefore categorised as **business-critical risk** at board level.

---

## 4. Business Impact of a Cyber Incident

Given the nature of financial services, a cybersecurity incident could have severe financial, operational, and regulatory consequences.

### Potential Impacts Include:

#### 1. Financial Fraud
- Unauthorised transactions
- Manipulated trading activity
- Direct financial loss to customers
- Compensation liabilities

#### 2. Regulatory Penalties
- APRA enforcement action
- Fines under Privacy Act
- Mandatory compliance remediation programs
- Increased regulatory scrutiny

#### 3. Loss of Customer Trust
- Customer churn
- Brand damage
- Negative media coverage
- Reduced investor confidence

#### 4. Shareholder & Market Impact
- Decline in company valuation
- Loss of institutional partnerships
- Increased cost of cyber insurance

#### 5. Operational Disruption
- Platform downtime
- Trading suspension
- Incident response costs
- Recovery and system rebuild expenses

In the financial sector, even short periods of service disruption can lead to significant financial and reputational consequences.

For this reason, cybersecurity risk management must be proactive, structured, and aligned with regulatory expectations.

---

## Strategic Objective of This Project

This project simulates the role of a Cyber Risk Analyst responsible for:

- Identifying critical assets
- Assessing cloud-related risks
- Evaluating security controls
- Measuring inherent and residual risk
- Aligning cloud controls with ISO 27001 and regulatory requirements
- Reporting cyber risk exposure to executive leadership

The objective is to ensure that the AWS migration does not increase overall business risk beyond acceptable tolerance levels.

Cybersecurity is treated not as a technical function alone, but as an enterprise risk management discipline.
